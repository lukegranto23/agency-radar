# Agency Radar Go-To-Market

## Best Initial Niche

Start with `Federal IT Services Radar`.

Why:

- buyers already spend on capture intelligence
- the award pool is large
- incumbent and expiring-contract data is commercially useful
- the buyer can justify a monthly fee quickly

## Who To Sell To

- small federal contractors
- subcontractors trying to team under primes
- capture managers
- proposal consultants
- niche MSPs and cybersecurity vendors trying to enter federal work

## What To Sell

Do not sell a dashboard.

Sell:

- a weekly intelligence brief
- a shortlist of expiring contracts
- top-spending agencies in the niche
- top incumbents to partner against
- a CSV export for BD teams

## Pricing

Start simple:

- `$99/month`: one weekly report, one niche
- `$249/month`: weekly report + custom watchlist + CSV
- `$499/month`: two niches + custom filters + Slack/email delivery

## Sales Angle

Use this line:

`I built a narrow federal spend monitor that shows who is winning contracts, which agencies are spending, and which awards are expiring soon in your niche.`

That is concrete. Avoid broad claims like "AI procurement platform."

## 20-Prospect Test

Before polishing more product:

1. Find 20 buyers in one niche.
2. Generate one sample report.
3. Send a tailored version to 5 of them.
4. If nobody replies, change the niche or the pitch before building more.

## Conversion Trigger

The product becomes easier to sell when you can say:

- "Here are 12 awards ending in the next 180 days."
- "Here are the agencies spending most in your NAICS."
- "Here are the incumbents you should team under."

That is why the current report emphasizes expiring awards and top recipients.
